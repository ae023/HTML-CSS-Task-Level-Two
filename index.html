Two aesthetics exist: the passive aesthetic of mirrors,
                      and the active aesthetic of prisms.

Guided by the former, art turns into a copy of the environment's objectivity,
                      or the individual's psychic history.

Guided by the latter, art is redeemed,
                      makes the world into its instrument,
                      and forges,
                      beyond spatial and temporal prisons,
                      a personal vision.
